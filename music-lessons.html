Philosophy

Notation and Tablature

These tune-specific notations are intended to serve as the most basic "in the box" notation of each tune. I believe that each player should adapt and embellish the arrangements as they see fit, both for preference of fingerboard positions as well as adding variations.

Almost every passage on the banjo can be done in a myriad ways when arranged in the pure "melodic" approach (using open or closed strings), the "single string" approach, or some combination therein.

In light of that, many players have noted the perils of simply memorizing tablature for harmonic tunes: you become reliant on the pages themselves to find a path that leads you the optimal result (the desired notes of a particular melody).

What I advocate is to deeply internalize the scale pattern shapes for any particular scale (major, diminished, harmonic minor etc.) so that you can read or listen to the target tune and be able to find the different possible harmonic patterns yourself from memory. This will still require practice and experimentation.
